\section*{18. Mixed Text and Math}

Let $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$ where $a \neq 0$. The roots are $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ and the vertex is at $\left(-\frac{b}{2a}, f\left(-\frac{b}{2a}\right)\right)$.

If $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n$ converges, then $\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 0$. The converse is false: $\sum \frac{1}{n}$ diverges even though $\frac{1}{n} \to 0$.
