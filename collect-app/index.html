Important — please read

The tabs at the top

  • Guide: this page. Always available for reference.
  • Collection: the drawing screen. The big handwritten character is your target. Draw it in the canvas, then hit Accept to save.
  • Manager: view and delete samples you've already collected for the current character.
  • Settings: switch between right-handed and left-handed layout, download all samples as JSON, or reset everything.

Picking a character (the sidebar)

The sidebar on the left (titled Characters) is how you choose what to collect.

  • Tap a category name to expand it. The chevron rotates to indicate open or closed.
  • Tap a character to make it the current target. It shows up as the big handwritten letter on the Collection tab.
  • The number next to a character is how many samples you've drawn for it. The number next to a category (e.g. 7/26) is how many of its characters you've started.
  • The icon at the top-left of the page hides or shows the sidebar. The sidebar flips to the right when you switch to left-handed mode in Settings.

Drawing controls

  • Accept: save the current strokes as a sample.
  • Undo: remove only the last stroke (not the whole drawing).
  • Clear: wipe the canvas.
  • Next: move to the next character in the current category. The site does not auto-advance, so collect as many variations as you want before moving on.

Priority: what to collect first

The categories in the sidebar are ordered top-to-bottom by priority. Work through them in that order. Don't sprawl across all of them at once.

Tier 1: Essential

These are very commonly used. Best to just do all of them.

  • Lowercase Letters
  • Uppercase Letters
  • Digits
  • Basic Punctuation: period, comma, semicolon, colon, exclamation, question mark
  • Quotes & Apostrophes
  • Brackets: ( ) [ ] { }
  • Special Characters: @ # $ % & ^ _ ~ | \

Tier 2: Math basics

Important for typeset math.

  • Math Variables (lowercase): italic math letters, rendered as a separate set from text letters
  • Math Variables (uppercase)
  • Math Operators: + − = × ÷ < >
  • Delimiters: bracket variants drawn at multiple heights so they scale cleanly around tall math (matrices, vectors, big fractions). Heights you don't draw fall back to Latin Modern.

Tier 3: Math symbols

Needed for most undergraduate-level math.

  • Math Symbols: ± × ÷ √ ∞ ∂ ∇ ∅ ∀ ∃ etc.
  • Binary Operators: ⊕ ⊗ ⊙ ⊖ ⊘ ⊻ ⊼ ⊽ (circled-plus is the XOR symbol)
  • Big Operators: Σ Π ∫ ∬ ∮
  • Greek Lowercase
  • Greek Uppercase
  • Relations: ≤ ≥ ≠ ≈ ≡ ⊂ ⊃ ∈

Tier 4: Optional

Skip unless you specifically need them.

  • Arrows
  • Dots & Misc: center dot, ellipsis, primes, degree

Tips for good samples

  • Use Apple Pencil if you have one. The site captures pressure, so Pencil samples produce richer strokes than finger drawings.
  • Vary your style. Draw the same letter a few times with slight differences (slant, size, openness). The font reads more natural with within-glyph variation than identical replicas.
  • Lift the pen between strokes. Letters like t and i need their crossbar or dot as a separate stroke.
  • Don't overthink. Aim for natural handwriting, not penmanship-class perfect.
  • Skip what you won't use. You don't have to draw every glyph. If you know your work will never need a particular character (e.g. niche Greek symbols, certain arrows), feel free to skip it. Missing characters fall back to a default in the rendered output.
